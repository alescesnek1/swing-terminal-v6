SWING TERMINAL · v5

The Manual.

Every signal on this terminal, explained like you're five.

🧠Why this terminal exists

Imagine 500 candy stores. Each store sells the same kind of candy but the prices move every second. This terminal is a giant glass wall showing all 500 candy stores at once, with little lights that turn on when something fun is happening.

  • COIN = one of the cryptocurrencies we watch.
  • SIGNAL = a label that says BUY / SHORT / FLUSH / WATCH.
  • SCORE = 0–10. Bigger = louder.

🎯Sniper Protocol

ELI5: A sniper is someone hiding with a really big toy buy-order. When they show up under the current price, it's like a giant trampoline: if the price falls there, it bounces back up.

Technically: we scan the order book for unusually large bid walls within striking distance of price. The terminal stamps the row with a 🎯 and tells you the wall size and distance.

  • Wall depth — how heavy the trampoline is.
  • Wall distance — how far you fall before it catches you.
  • Trigger — close + heavy = green light.

🚪Venue Gate

ELI5: Not every candy store is real. Some look real but the candy is plastic. Venue Gate is a bouncer who checks the ID of every coin before it gets into the club.

Every coin gets a badge after the bouncer is done:

BIN Real candy, sold at Binance Spot. Deepest liquidity.
ALPHA Sold only at the futures counter. Riskier but tradable.
DEX Sold in the back-alley shop. Smaller and bumpier.

The bouncer rejects a coin from BIN/ALPHA unless: symbol matches exactly, the pair is live in the ticker right now, prices on both sides line up within 1.5×, and market cap is ≥ $50M. Anyone failing any check → DEX.

🌊Volatility & Hotness

ELI5: Volatility is how jumpy the room is. If kids are bouncing off walls, that's volatile. Hotness is how popular the room is right now — many kids running in.
  • Hotness — 0 = empty room, 100 = packed. >80 is EXTREME.
  • Volatility badge — counts how many coins are jumping unusually hard right now.
  • Regime upgrade — too much jumping = the whole market is shaky.

💧FLUSH

ELI5: FLUSH = everyone is screaming and selling at the same time — like when one kid drops their candy and suddenly everyone drops theirs too.

A coin is flagged FLUSH when:

  • 24h change is deeply red (strong drawdown).
  • Volume is way above the recent average (capitulation volume).
  • 1H is still falling — knife is not done falling yet.

FLUSH is not a buy. It's a "step back, the knife is moving" warning. Snipers wait for it to stop falling before reaching in.

🟢BUY

ELI5: The candy got cheaper, more kids are showing up, and the line outside the shop is growing. The shop is opening, not closing.
  • Price is trending up on multiple timeframes.
  • Volume is rising with the price (real demand).
  • Open interest is climbing — futures traders are leaning long.
  • Score ≥ 7 → STRONG BUY. Score 6 = WATCH.

🔴SHORT

ELI5: The shop owner is sneaking out the back door with all the candy. Everyone outside still thinks the shop is open, but it's about to close.
  • Price is breaking support.
  • Volume is rising into the breakdown (not a fakeout).
  • Funding is positive but OI is dropping — longs giving up.

SHORT = "betting price goes lower." You're renting candy, selling it, planning to buy it back cheaper.

⚖️LONGS vs SHORTS

ELI5: LONGS bet candy goes up. SHORTS bet candy goes down. They sit on opposite ends of a seesaw and the price moves toward whoever is heavier.
  • Open Interest (OI) — total weight on the seesaw.
  • Funding — a tax that pushes the seesaw back toward balance.
  • Taker ratio — which side is impatient and slamming the market.

When LONGS are crowded + funding is hot + price stops going up → squeeze risk. Same with SHORTS in reverse.

🌦️Regime

ELI5: The weather. Sunny = BULL. Rain = BEAR. Drizzle = CHOP. You wear different clothes in different weather. Same with trading.
  • BULL / TREND (score > 65)
  • CHOP (35–65)
  • BEAR / FLUSH (< 35)

🗺️Heatmap

ELI5: A map of the candy kingdom. Each rectangle is a candy store. Bigger rectangle = bigger store. Green = price went up today. Red = price went down today.
  • Size = market cap.
  • Color = 24h move.
  • Hover for details, click to drill in.

PANIC INDICATOR — MANUAL

How it is calculated

Composite mathematical blend of three orthogonal pressure signals:

  • 50 % — 24-hour rolling Volume Delta (cohort-relative spike magnitude, signed by price direction).
  • 30 % — 1-hour Price Velocity (signed percentage change over the last hour).
  • 20 % — Institutional Orderbook Sniping anomalies (bid-wall proximity × wall confidence).

panic = 0.5 · sign(c1h) · |Δvol24h| + 0.3 · c1h + 0.2 · sniperprox

Result clamps to −100 … +100. 0 = neutral, no quantitative edge. Until live WebSocket frames arrive, a static proxy seeded from cohort-relative 24h volatility and volume z-score keeps the column populated.

How to trade it

+80 … +100  ·  Extreme FOMO

Peak retail FOMO. High risk of an immediate local blow-off top. Consider taking profits or scaling out of leveraged longs. Do NOT chase market-orders here.

+20 … +79  ·  Mild Buying Pressure

Crowd is bid-side. Trend-follow only with confirmation from SCORE ≥ 6 (BUY label) and a supportive market REGIME.

−19 … +19  ·  Neutral

No quantitative panic edge. Trade on structure, not on this number.

−79 … −20  ·  Mild Panic Selling

Crowd is dumping. SHORT setups valid with SCORE ≥ 6 (SHORT label). Do NOT catch a falling knife on spot.

−100 … −80  ·  Capitulation / Panic Sell

Blood in the streets. High statistical probability of a sharp mean-reversion bounce. Prime zone for sniper long entries or staged spot accumulation — size with the regime-appropriate risk envelope.
